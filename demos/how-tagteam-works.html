How TagTeam.js Works

From everyday sentences to structured semantic graphs

Input: Plain English Sentence
"The doctor must allocate the last ventilator between two critically ill patients"
POSTagger (jsPOS Lexicon)

📚 Word Classification

Every word is labeled with its grammatical type using a 4-million-word dictionary. This tells us which words are nouns (people, things), verbs (actions), modals (obligation words like "must"), and more.

The DET doctor NOUN must MODAL allocate VERB the DET last ADJ ventilator NOUN between PREP two critically ill ADJ patients NOUN
1
EntityExtractor + RealWorldEntityFactory

🔍 Two-Tier Entity Extraction

We create two layers: Discourse Referents (the exact phrases from the text) and Real-World Entities (the actual people/things they refer to). The referent is_about the real entity.

Tier 1: Discourse Referents (Text Phrases)
💬 "The doctor"
💬 "the last ventilator"
💬 "two critically ill patients"
Tier 2: Real-World Entities
👤 Person: doctor
🛠 Artifact: ventilator
👥 ObjectAggregate: 2 patients
"The doctor" → is_about → Person: doctor
2
ActExtractor + RoleDetector + QualityFactory + DirectiveExtractor

🎲 Acts, Roles, Qualities & Directives

Verbs become Acts. People bear Roles (agent, patient). Descriptive words create Qualities. Modal words like "must" create Directives that prescribe the act.

⚡ ActOfAllocation (allocate)
📜 DirectiveContent (must)
🎭 AgentRole
🎭 PatientRole (x3)
✔ DiseaseQuality (critically ill)
⚠ ScarcityAssertion (last)
3
SemanticGraphBuilder

🔗 Connecting with Predicates

All nodes are connected using formal predicates from BFO and CCO ontologies. Each relationship has precise meaning that machines can reason about.

ActOfAllocation → has_agent → Person: doctor
ActOfAllocation → affects → Artifact: ventilator
ActOfAllocation → has_participant → ObjectAggregate
DirectiveContent → prescribes → ActOfAllocation
AgentRole → inheres_in → Person: doctor
AgentRole → would_be_realized_in → ActOfAllocation
Person: doctor → is_bearer_of → AgentRole
DiseaseQuality → inheres_in → Person: patient 1
ObjectAggregate → has_member → Person: patient 1, 2
ScarcityAssertion → is_about → Artifact: ventilator
4
Output: JSON-LD Semantic Graph

The final result is a structured, machine-readable graph in JSON-LD format that captures who (agents), what action (acts), who/what is affected (patients), obligations (directives), conditions (qualities), and constraints (scarcity).

💬 Discourse Referents linked to real entities
👤 Persons with Roles they bear
⚡ Acts with agents and participants
📜 Directives that prescribe acts
✔ Qualities that inhere in entities
⚠ Scarcity assertions about resources

Key Predicates (Relationships)

is_about Links a discourse referent to the real-world entity it refers to
has_agent Links an act to the person performing it
affects Links an act to the entity directly affected by it
has_participant Links an act to all entities involved in it
prescribes Links a directive to the act it obligates
inheres_in Links a role or quality to the entity that has it
is_bearer_of Links an entity to the roles it carries
would_be_realized_in Links a role to the act where it would be exercised
has_member Links an aggregate (group) to its individual members
is_concretized_by Links information content to the text that carries it
describes_quality Links a discourse referent to qualities mentioned about it
extracted_from Links derived content to its source discourse referent
